## Business Overview

### The Opportunity

The Smithsonian Open Access initiative released **4.5+ million images** into the public domain. Most people don't know this exists, and even fewer know how to use these assets commercially. We're bridging that gap by:

1. Curating high-quality artwork from the Smithsonian collection
2. Processing images for commercial use (clean backgrounds, high-res, organized)
3. Packaging into themed bundles designers actually want
4. Distributing across multiple sales channels via Commerce Hub

### Value Proposition

**For Customers:**
- Ready-to-use vintage artwork (no hunting through archives)
- Professionally processed (transparent PNGs, 300 DPI)
- Themed collections (saves hours of curation)
- Commercial license included (public domain = no attribution required)

**For Us:**
- Zero inventory costs (digital products)
- Infinite margin after creation (no COGS)
- Multi-channel distribution (one product â†’ many stores)
- Evergreen content (vintage never goes out of style)

## Product Strategy

#### 1. Clipart Bundles ($8.99 - $14.99)
Themed collections of 40-60 processed images.

**Processing Standard:**
- Transparent PNG format
- 300 DPI minimum
- Background removed (where applicable)
- Color corrected
- Organized in labeled folders

## Pricing Strategy

### Digital Downloads

| Tier | Price | Value Proposition |
|------|-------|-------------------|
| Single Bundle | $8.99 | Entry point, impulse buy |
| Bundle + Bonus | $12.99 | Better value perception |
| Mega Bundle | $29.99 | "Get everything" buyers |

**Psychology:**
- $8.99 under the $10 psychological barrier
- Odd pricing (.99) feels like a deal
- Mega bundle at 3x single price = perceived savings

### Platform-Specific Pricing

| Platform | Price | Reason |

## Marketing Strategy

### Channel Priority

1. **Pinterest** (Primary discovery)
2. **Etsy** (Primary sales)
3. **Instagram** (Brand building)
4. **Email** (Retention)
5. **SEO/Blog** (Long-term organic)

### Pinterest Strategy

**Why Pinterest:**
- 450M+ monthly active users
- 85% use Pinterest for purchase planning
- Visual search perfect for art/design products
- Pins have long shelf life (months vs hours on social)
- Direct link to product pages

**Content Plan:**
- 5-10 pins daily (scheduled via Tailwind)
- Mix of product pins and inspirational content
- Create boards for each product category
- Use idea pins for tutorials/behind-the-scenes

**Pin Types:**
| Type | Purpose | Ratio |
|------|---------|-------|
| Product Pins | Direct sales | 40% |
| Mockup Pins | Show use cases | 30% |
| Inspiration Pins | Build following | 20% |
| Tutorial Pins | Establish expertise | 10% |

**Keyword Strategy:**
- "vintage clipart"
- "botanical illustrations"
- "nautical clipart commercial use"

- "scrapbook supplies digital"
- "wedding invitation graphics"

### Etsy Strategy

**Why Etsy:**

**Optimization Checklist:**

## Competitive Analysis

### Direct Competitors

| Competitor | Strengths | Weaknesses | Our Advantage |
|------------|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Creative Market | Huge selection | Expensive, overwhelming | Curated, themed bundles |
| Design Bundles | Great deals | Quality varies | Consistent museum quality |
| The Hungry JPEG | Cheap bundles | Generic content | Unique historical art |
| Etsy sellers | Established trust | Inconsistent quality | Professional processing |

### Positioning

We're not competing on:
- Price (race to bottom)
- Volume (can't match big marketplaces)

We're competing on:
- **Curation** (we find the gems so you don't have to)
- **Quality** (museum-grade, professionally processed)
- **Story** (real art history, not generic vectors)
- **Uniqueness** (Smithsonian collection = exclusive feel)
